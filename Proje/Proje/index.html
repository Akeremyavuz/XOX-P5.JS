TIC TAC TOE

Father of Games

XOX, developed by Alexander Sandy Douglas in 1952, is the world's first video game. Douglas created the game while he was a doctoral student at the University of Cambridge for his doctoral thesis. The game is a tic-tac-toe game developed for the EDSAC computer. It is a paper-and-pencil game played by two people. An interface is provided with a 35x26 pixel video device. Since EDSAC was a university-specific computer, it did not spread to the world.
